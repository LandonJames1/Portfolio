Executive Summary

For this project I decided to conduct some marketing research analysis on a dataset consisting of customer experience surveys with over 100,000 respondents. The dataset encompasses a range of factors including demographics (such as gender and age), travel preferences (like customer type and type of travel), and service evaluations (e.g., seat comfort, cleanliness, and inflight entertainment). Each satisfaction metric, except for the overall satisfaction rating, is graded on a scale of 1 to 5. Through rigorous data analysis, insights were drawn regarding the factors influencing overall customer satisfaction. Key findings revealed correlations between specific amenities, such as inflight entertainment and seat comfort, and overall satisfaction levels. Additionally, the analysis identified potential areas for improvement in airline services, offering valuable insights for enhancing customer experiences and loyalty.

Marketing Problem

The marketing problem addressed in this analysis revolves around helping airlines gain deeper insights into their customers preferences and experiences. By understanding these needs and wants more comprehensively, airlines can tailor their services to better meet customer expectations, ultimately enhancing overall satisfaction. Moreover, optimizing marketing strategies based on these insights enables airlines to effectively target their audience segments, improving customer acquisition and retention efforts. Additionally, by identifying areas of strength and weakness in service provision, airlines can strategically allocate resources to enhance the quality of offerings where necessary and streamline expenditures where demand may be lower, leading to more efficient resource management. Overall, the objective is to empower airlines with the knowledge needed to optimize their operations, marketing initiatives, and resource allocation strategies in alignment with customer preferences and market dynamics.

One plausible explanation could be that customers tend to book their flights well in advance, and any challenges they encountered during the booking process may have become less relevant by the time they completed the survey.

K-mean Clustering

train_copy <- select(train, -id, -`...1`)

train_encoded <- dummy_cols(train_copy, select_columns = c("Gender", "Customer Type", "Type of Travel", "Class", "satisfaction"), remove_first_dummy = TRUE)

numerical_train_encoded <- select(train_encoded, where(is.numeric))

numerical_train_imputed <- numerical_train_encoded %>%
  mutate(across(everything(), ~ifelse(is.na(.), mean(., na.rm = TRUE), .)))

set.seed(100)
kmeans_result <- kmeans(numerical_train_imputed, centers = 5, nstart = 25, iter.max = 500)

train_copy$Cluster <- kmeans_result$cluster

cluster_summary <- train_copy %>%
  group_by(Cluster) %>%
  summarise(across(c("Age", "Gender", "Customer_Type", "Type_of_Travel", "Class", "satisfaction"), mean, na.rm = TRUE))

write_csv(train_copy, "clustered_data.csv")

numerical_train_matrix <- as.matrix(numerical_train_imputed)

satisfied_counts <- train_copy %>%

Summary of Marketing Insights

The marketing research analysis conducted on the vast dataset of airline customer experience surveys, encompassing over 100,000 respondents, has revealed significant insights into factors influencing overall satisfaction levels. Through rigorous data analysis, correlations between various amenities and overall satisfaction have been uncovered. For instance, amenities like inflight entertainment and seat comfort exhibit robust positive correlations with overall satisfaction, emphasizing the pivotal role of onboard services in shaping passengers perceptions of their travel experiences. Surprisingly, gate location satisfaction demonstrated little consistent impact on overall satisfaction, suggesting potential misconceptions in customer priorities.
Furthermore, the analysis delved into the impact of ease of online booking on overall satisfaction levels. Contrary to initial expectations, both the highest and lowest ease of booking scores corresponded to the highest overall satisfaction scores. This unexpected finding underscores the nuanced nature of customer satisfaction, suggesting that factors beyond the booking process may significantly influence passengers overall satisfaction levels. Additionally, the logistic regression model, achieving an 82% accuracy rate in predicting customer satisfaction, offers airlines a powerful tool to proactively address customer concerns and tailor services to enhance overall satisfaction levels.
In addition to these findings, the k-means clustering analysis yielded valuable insights into customer segmentation. The clustering identified five distinct customer clusters based on demographic and satisfaction-related attributes. For example, Cluster 1 consists predominantly of experienced business travelers with relatively high satisfaction levels, while Cluster 5 comprises younger individuals with slightly lower satisfaction scores. Understanding these clusters enables airlines to tailor services and marketing strategies to meet the unique needs and preferences of different customer segments. Moreover, the logistic regression coefficients for demographic variables provide actionable insights into the factors influencing customer satisfaction. For instance, Type_of_Travel emerged as the most impactful variable, highlighting the significant influence of business travel on overall satisfaction levels. By leveraging these insights, airlines can optimize resource allocation, target marketing efforts effectively, and ultimately enhance overall customer satisfaction, loyalty, and business performance in the competitive airline industry.
